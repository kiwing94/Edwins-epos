Cultural Context

Aramaic was the **lingua franca** of the Near East during the time of Jesus and his disciples. It was spoken widely across the region, including in **Palestine**. Aramaic held a sacred role as the language of the **Old Testament**, and Jesus himself spoke Aramaic, as did many early Christians. The significance of Aramaic is evident in its direct connection to many **biblical passages** and its usage in the **Gospels**.

Aramaic's script and sound remain an important cultural marker, uniting the **Hebrew and Arabic** traditions while preserving its own unique beauty.
